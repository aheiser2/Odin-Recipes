Flerzgalleiolzs With Qurrambix-Sauce

Description

Text

Ingredients

  • Several muyty leaves
  • 4 cups of water
  • 2 cups of jargaz'tn meelk
  • 2 tbsp zkuralz powder
  • 2 tbsp tarx powder
  • 1 tbsp crushed plarkql
  • 1 tsp red qurruz powder, or to taste

Steps

To make the Flerzgalleiolzs:
  1. Begin by placing the gleurts in the water to soak, for at least 6 hours. You may want to leave them overnight for best results - however, do not leave longer than a day as they can hatch if left in water too long (hatched gleruts are poisonous (and also venomous!)).
  2. After the gleurts have lost their color in the water, it should be smoking nicely and have taken on a bright blue hue. Pour the water slowly through a sive into a large bowl, collecting all of the gleurts in the sive. Be sure to dispose of the gleurts properly so that they do not hatch in your local water supply.
To make the Qurrambix-Sauce:
  1. Add the kukuurix powder, the tarx powder, and the plrubus seeds. You should notice a thickening of the mixture right away - the seeds are fast acting and will dissolve, but it is imperative you do not touch them while they are working. Wait 10 minutes (15 may be better if your seeds are older, as they get more deadly with age), then gently shake the bowl to allow the mix to begin to coagulate well and for the kukuurix and tarx flavors to permeate the gelatinous mixture.
  2. Now that it's taken on a Jello-like look, gently drizzle on the huourgtzel syrup and allow it to soak into the form. This should take a few minutes, but the gleuuurffz should take on an even brighter blue hue from being fed the syrup, and will become more complacent about your next steps.
  3. Taking your chopped huourgtzels, gently push them one at a time into the body of the gleuuurffz. DO NOT push more than one in at a time - this will enrage the gleuuurffz, and cause it to turn a level of sour that the human mind cannot comprehend. Try to spread the huourgtzels out evenly, so that when biting into the gleuuurffz later, you do not end up with parts that are overfilled or empty of their spongey texture.
  4. The gleuuurffz is now ready to serve - using the sharpest knife you have, cut it into several pieces, even if you are only planning to eat one right now (this is done to afford the gleuuurffz as little pain as possible). If the piece tastes too sour, you may add human sugar to the top.
